Prolog is a logic programming language in which programs are written using facts and rules, and answers are obtained by asking queries.

How Prolog Works

Prolog works with three things:

Facts – Information that is true.
Rules – Logical relationships.
Queries – Questions asked to the program.
Example

Fact:

likes(ankit, pizza).
likes(rahul, burger).

This means:

Ankit likes pizza.
Rahul likes burger.

Query:

?- likes(ankit, pizza).

Output:

Yes.
Example with Rules
parent(ram, shyam).
parent(shyam, hari).

grandparent(X, Y) :- parent(X, Z), parent(Z, Y).

Query:

?- grandparent(ram, hari).

Output:

Yes.

Because:

Ram is Shyam's parent.
Shyam is Hari's parent.
Therefore, Ram is Hari's grandparent.


Features of Prolog
Logic-based programming language.
Uses facts, rules, and queries.
No need to write the exact steps to solve a problem.
Commonly used in Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Expert Systems.



Applications of Prolog
Artificial Intelligence (AI)
Expert systems
Natural language processing
Theorem proving
Knowledge-based systems


Easy way to remember
C/C++: Tell the computer how to solve the problem.
Prolog: Tell the computer what is true (facts and rules), and it figures out how to answer the query.
